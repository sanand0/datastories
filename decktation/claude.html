Interview Transcript

Consultant

Hey, thanks for making the time. I know you're slammed. I'll keep this tight. To start, when you say "client reporting," what exactly are we talking about—quarterly reports for which products?

Ops Manager

Yep, quarterly client reports for our equity funds. So, commingled equity vehicles, a couple of separate accounts that follow the same reporting pack. Same rhythm every quarter: compile, review, distribute.

Consultant

Got it. Walk me through the core workflow end-to-end. Like, "data comes from X, then Y happens, then PDF goes out."

Ops Manager

Sure. Step one: we request the quarter-end data from our third-party fund administrator—Vistara. They send back what they call the "reporting bundle." Step two: we reconcile that against our internal numbers, basically to make sure holdings, cash, fees, performance, all line up. Step three: we populate our report templates—these are formatting-heavy PDF templates—with the validated numbers. Step four: IR reviews commentary and sign-off. Step five: we send to clients and upload to the portal.

Consultant

When you say "reporting bundle," what format is it coming in?

Ops Manager

That's… the pain point. It's raw and unstructured. Some parts are Excel extracts, some are PDFs, sometimes it's a ZIP with multiple versions, and the naming conventions aren't consistent. You'll see "Final_FINAL2" kind of stuff. And then we have to manually reconcile it.

Consultant

So the bottleneck is the intake and reconciliation from Vistara, before it even hits your templates?

Ops Manager

Exactly. If Vistara gave us clean, structured tables with stable field names, we'd be in a different world. Instead, we spend days interpreting what they meant, matching it to our internal ledger, and then copy-pasting into the templates.

Consultant

Let's quantify it. What's the current effort and headcount?

Ops Manager

It's three FTEs dedicated for about two full weeks every quarter. That's basically their life during reporting season—data collation, reconciliation, and formatting. And it's not just "busy." It's brittle. One person out sick and we feel it immediately.

Consultant

Three FTEs, two full weeks, every quarter—just for data collation and formatting. Not including review cycles?

Ops Manager

Correct. Review cycles add time, but the heavy lift is getting the numbers into the right shape and into the PDF templates. The templates are… I mean, they're a nightmare. Very brand-driven. Lots of locked formatting, tables that break if you breathe on them.

Consultant

Where do handoffs happen? I'm listening for "this team does X, then throws over the wall."

Ops Manager

Vistara to FundOps is the big one. Then FundOps to IR for commentary and final checks. Sales also gets pulled in because they want certain client-specific angles, but that's more ad hoc. And Compliance sometimes pops in late with "did you include the right disclosures."

Consultant

Quick clarification: is the commentary written by FundOps or IR?

Ops Manager

Mostly IR. But we feed them a draft, honestly. We provide the standard paragraphs, the "market backdrop," that sort of thing, because they're also stretched.

Consultant

And quality-wise—what's working, what isn't?

Ops Manager

Look, our primary goal is getting the reports out the door on time. That's the KPI that matters. But yes—IR and Sales complain a lot that the commentary is repetitive and lacks analytical depth. They'll say, "This reads like last quarter with the dates changed." And… they're not wrong. We don't have time to do deep analysis when we're fighting formatting issues.

Consultant

Understood. Where do errors creep in—if they do?

Ops Manager

Manual copy-paste is the obvious one. Also version control. Vistara sends updates, then we're not always sure which number set made it into which template version. We've got checklists, but it's still messy.

Consultant

If I waved a wand, what would you change first?

Ops Manager

Standardize the intake from Vistara. Give me structured data, predictable fields, and a reconciliation report that ties out cleanly. Second, reduce dependency on these PDF templates—at least automate population. Because right now, three people spending two weeks per quarter on collation and formatting is just… not a good use of talent.

Consultant

Last one: what does "success" look like a year from now?

Ops Manager

Reports out on time without heroics. Maybe one FTE doing oversight instead of three people in the trenches. And commentary that actually says something new—so IR and Sales stop rolling their eyes.

Hidden Risk
Manual Copy-Paste + Version Confusion = Numbers You Can't Verify
Copy-Paste Error Risk

Manual transcription of numbers from Vistara extracts into PDF templates. Fat-finger errors, transposed digits, or missed cells are undetected until client review — or not at all. No automated validation layer exists.

Version Confusion Risk

Vistara periodically sends updated extracts mid-cycle. Without a version-controlled pipeline, teams cannot confirm which data set populated which template version — or which report went to which client.

Compliance & Audit Implication

Solution
Two Fixes Unlock a 66% FTE Reduction and Better-Quality Reports
1
Structured Data Intake from Vistara
  • Require Vistara to deliver a clean, stable-schema data package — not a mixed ZIP of Excel/PDFs
  • Define agreed field names, data types, and delivery format (e.g. API or standardized CSV/JSON)
  • Auto-generate a reconciliation report that ties Vistara data to the internal ledger with a pass/fail flag
  • Eliminate "Final_FINAL2" naming guesswork — version-stamped at source
  • Reduce intake & reconciliation from days to hours
2
Automated Template Population
  • Programmatic PDF/report generation from validated data source — no manual copy-paste
  • Each report generation run is logged: data version → template version → output file → client
  • Enables full audit trail answering "which numbers went to which client, when"
  • Frees formatting-constrained templates: same data, multiple output formats (PDF, web, board pack)
  • Analyst time shifts from copy-paste to genuine analysis and commentary
