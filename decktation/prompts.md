## Transcript generation

<!-- Source: https://gemini.google.com/app/388e5b93f03815b1 -->

Act as a seasoned Private Equity Operations Manager and a polite but probing Consultant. Generate a realistic, 600-word transcript of a discovery interview focusing on the "Client Reporting" process in FundOps.

**The Characters:**

- **Consultant:** Asking targeted questions about workflow, bottlenecks, headcount, and data handoffs.
- **Ops Manager:** Slightly stretched for time, highly focused on operational efficiency, but yields detailed, practical answers.

**Key Information to Embed in the Conversation:**

- **The Core Process:** Compiling and distributing quarterly client reports for equity funds.
- **The Bottleneck:** Receiving raw, unstructured data from the third-party fund administrator (Vistara) and manually reconciling it before copying it into formatting-heavy PDF templates.
- **The Metrics:** It currently takes a dedicated team of 3 FTEs roughly two full weeks every quarter just to handle data collation and formatting.
- **The Quality Gap:** The Ops Manager should mention that their primary goal is just getting the reports out the door on time, but admit that the Investor Relations (IR) and Sales teams frequently complain that the commentary text is repetitive and lacks analytical depth.

**Tone and Style:**

- Make it sound like a real, slightly messy Zoom call. Include natural conversational pivots, a brief clarification, and realistic corporate phrasing.
- Ensure the operational data points are clear enough that a secondary LLM could easily extract them to build a structured process flow diagram or HTML summary.

## Generate slides (Codex - gpt-5.3-codex - xhigh)

Convert transcript.md into a beautiful responsive slide deck, McKinsey style with action titles. Just reading the titles should give the audience the entire message of the deck.
Follow the pyramid principle. The contents of the slide should prove the title.
The deck should summarize the key points of the transcript, helping the audience understand what the interviewer intended to learn the process.
Make the slides content rich, i.e. clear and self-explanatory with enough detail to help the audience understand without a narrator.
Visual quality: is critical. Use consistency, bold typography, contrast, visual hierarchy, progressive disclosure, repetition, alignment, information density calibration, and other principles of visual design - while also evaluating relevant visual format innovation. Use professional iconography, typography, stock images, etc. as appropriate.
Use animated SVGs to explain the processes, mechanisms, workflows, etc. The aim is to make users FEEL the process. One glance should give them an intuitive understanding of how it works, even before they read the accompanying text. Show how things are connected, what data flows from where to where, how elements, interact, etc.
Write as a single page HTML application in index.html.

Before the first page, introduce a process slide. Mention that this deck is auto-generated by transcribing recording.opus (allow user to play the audio element) which was automatically transcribed into transcript.md (clicking should open the transcript in a popup) and automatically converted into this slide deck. Depict this process visually with an animated SVG. Explain the benefits of such an automated process from a business perspective. The obvious is saved time and money, but also mention less obvious benefits such as: scalability, consistency, the ability to easily update the deck when the process changes, the ability to generate multiple versions of the deck for different audiences (e.g. IR vs Sales), etc.

After that slide, progress to the actual content slides summarizing the transcript.

Plan like an expert writing the slides. In this context, first think about:

- What patterns would an expert in this field check / recognize that beginners would miss?
- What questions would an expert ask that a beginner would not know to?
- What problems / failures would an expert anticipate that beginners may not be aware of?

Use this to formulate a plan of action before writing the slides.
